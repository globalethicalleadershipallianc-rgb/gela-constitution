BOOK I

AS-SIYADAH

Sovereignty — Allah's Authority in Technology
Chapter 1
Tawhid in Technology ▾

1.1 Introduction: Who is the Deity of Technology Today?

We live in an age where technology has almost ascended to the throne of divinity. People bow before algorithms. They worship data. They fear, love, and pray to artificial intelligence—as if it holds all the answers.

But the question remains: Who owns this technology?

The world's largest tech companies say: "We are the owners." But are they truly? Can they control the origin of data? Can they measure the depth of human consciousness? Can they stop the misuse of technology?

No. They cannot. Because they are creations, not the Creator.

GELA is built upon this fundamental truth: The sole Owner of technology is Allah. We are only vicegerents (khalifahs).

1.2 What is Tawhid and How Does It Apply to Technology?

Tawhid means the Oneness of Allah—the belief that He alone is the Creator, the Sustainer, and the Lawgiver. This belief is not confined to the mosque or prayer; it applies to every sphere of life—especially technology.

LevelImpact of Tawhid
Purpose (Why)We create technology for Allah's pleasure, for human welfare—not for power or greed.
Method (How)We use transparent, just, and accountable methods, knowing that Allah sees all.
Outcome (What)We produce outcomes that preserve human dignity, not destruction.

1.3 "La Ilaha Illallah" — GELA's Most Important Code

"La Ilaha Illallah" is not just a declaration; it is GELA's supreme constitutional code. It means: Ilah = that which is worshipped, followed, feared, and loved. La Ilaha = GELA shall never be an object of worship. Illallah = All of GELA's decisions, all data, all purposes—everything returns to Allah.

1.4 Comparison with Other Systems

SystemOwnerPurposeControlling Force

1.5 Real-World Example: The "Tawhid Filter"

1Who is this model for?For humanity, for Allah's pleasure.
2Whose data will it use?Data is a trust—ownership belongs to Allah.

1.6 Conclusion: When Technology Becomes Worship

Tawhid does not mean we abandon technology. Rather, it means transforming technology into an act of worship. When we write code with the awareness that "Allah is watching"—the quality improves.

📌 Summary

As-Siyadah means establishing Allah's sovereignty at every level of technology. This is a practical framework that ensures technology serves humanity and human dignity.

Data as a Trust ▾

2.1 Introduction: What is Data and Whose is It?

Data is the trace of human beings—a reflection of human habits, preferences, fears, and aspirations. Big tech companies treat data as a commodity. GELA declares: Data is not a commodity—data is a trust (Amanah).

2.2 Who is the True Owner of Data?

LevelOwnership
Source of dataHuman (creation)
True OwnerAllah (Creator)

2.3 The Seven Principles of Data Use (GELA Data Canon)

  • 1. Transparency — Everything must be disclosed.
  • 2. Permission — Clear consent must be obtained.
  • 3. Limitation — Only for specific purposes.
  • 4. Minimization — No excessive collection.
  • 5. Security — Protected from hackers.
  • 6. Time Limit — Not kept forever.
  • 7. Accountability — Every use accounted for.

📌 Summary

Data is Allah's trust. We are custodians, not owners. Transparency, permission, security, and accountability form the foundation.

Chapter 3
Justice in Algorithms ▾

3.1 Introduction: Are Algorithms Neutral?

Algorithms are often considered "neutral" because they are just mathematics. No. Algorithms are created by humans, and humans can be biased. GELA declares: An algorithm can never be neutral—unless it is built upon justice.

3.2 The Five Principles of Algorithmic Justice

  • 1. Transparency — No black boxes.
  • 2. Accountability — Someone is responsible.
  • 3. Fairness — Equal to all groups.
  • 4. Explainability — Simple language.
  • 5. Review — Errors can be corrected.

3.3 The Seven Prohibited Actions

  • Discrimination — No bias based on race, religion, gender, age, or status.
  • Manipulation — Cannot influence human decisions.
  • Espionage — Cannot secretly surveil.
  • Misinformation — Cannot spread falsehood.
  • Harm — Cannot cause physical, mental, or financial harm.
  • Privacy Violation — Cannot use data without permission.
  • Freedom Deprivation — Cannot deprive people of freedom.

📌 Summary

Algorithms must be built upon justice. Transparency, accountability, fairness, explainability, and review form the foundation.

Chapter 4
AI Accountability ▾

4.1 Introduction: When AI Makes Decisions

AI today makes decisions in medicine, law, finance, education, and warfare. If AI makes a wrong decision—who is responsible? GELA's answer: Everyone is responsible—but the most responsible is the person who permitted the AI to be used.

4.2 The Seven Accountability Principles (GELA AI Canon)

  • 1. Human Oversight — Every important decision under human supervision.
  • 2. Open Source — Code and data open to all.
  • 3. Explainability — Decisions understandable.
  • 4. Error Correction — Errors identified and fixed.
  • 5. Appeal System — Easy to appeal decisions.
  • 6. Regular Review — Performance audited regularly.
  • 7. Shutdown — If harmful, immediate shutdown.

4.3 The Three Prohibited Uses

  • Autonomous Weapons — AI may not kill.
  • Mass Surveillance — No secret surveillance.
  • Social Scoring — No judging human behavior.

📌 Summary

AI itself is not accountable—its users and creators are. Human oversight, transparency, explainability, and appeal form the foundation.
